Who We Are

Rooted in Tradition.
Forged in Armenia.

The JKA Armenia Shotokan Karate-Do Federation is the official representative of the Japan Karate Association (JKA) in Armenia. Founded with a commitment to preserving the authentic lineage of Shotokan as developed by Master Gichin Funakoshi, we bring world-class instruction to practitioners across the country.

Our federation operates multiple dojos throughout Yerevan and the regions, offering structured programs for children, adults, and competitive athletes. Every instructor is JKA-certified and trained in Japan, ensuring the highest standards of technique and ethical conduct are upheld in every class.

We believe karate is not merely a sport—it is a lifelong path of self-discovery, discipline, and respect. Whether you step on the floor for the first time or compete at the national level, JKA Armenia walks that path with you.

What We Offer

Three Pillars of Practice

⚡
Kihon — 基本

Fundamentals are the foundation of everything. We drill stances, blocks, strikes, and kicks with precision and repetition, building muscle memory that lasts a lifetime.

🌊
Kata — 型

The living library of karate. Each of the 26 JKA kata encodes centuries of combat wisdom. Through kata practice, students learn bunkai, timing, and the essence of the art.

🔥
Kumite — 組手

Controlled sparring tests everything learned in kihon and kata against a resisting partner. From basic ippon kumite to free-sparring, students develop courage, strategy, and composure under pressure.

🏆
Competition

JKA Armenia fields competitive teams at national and international tournaments. Athletes receive specialized coaching in both kata and kumite divisions under WKF-approved rules.

🧒
Children's Program

A structured curriculum designed for ages 5–14 focuses on coordination, confidence, focus, and respect—qualities that extend far beyond the dojo into school and everyday life.

🎌
JKA Certification

All grades from 9th kyu to 1st dan and above are recognized by JKA Headquarters in Tokyo. Your rank travels with you—accepted worldwide within the JKA network.

📜 History
Origins — 1993

JKA Armenia was founded in 1993 following the independence of the Republic of Armenia. The founding members had studied Shotokan Karate during the Soviet era and sought to establish an official, internationally recognized federation affiliated with the Japan Karate Association.

First National Championship — 1996

The first JKA Armenia National Championship was held in Yerevan in 1996, drawing over 200 competitors from dojos across the country. This marked the federation's recognition as the premier Shotokan body in Armenia.

International Affiliation — 2001

In 2001, JKA Armenia received full official membership from JKA Headquarters in Tokyo, under the authority of Chief Instructor Mikio Yahara. This allowed Armenian practitioners to hold internationally recognized JKA rank certificates.

European Presence — 2005–Present

Since 2005, JKA Armenia has sent competitors to the European JKA Championships, earning multiple bronze and silver medals in both kata and kumite. The federation continues to grow, with new dojos opening across Gyumri, Vanadzor, and Abovyan.

Today

With over 500 active members, 12 black-belt holders, and a nationally certified teaching faculty, JKA Armenia stands as one of the most respected martial arts organizations in the South Caucasus region.

🎽 Students
Belt Grade System

JKA Shotokan follows a traditional kyu/dan grading system. Grading exams are held every 3–6 months under certified JKA examiners.

White Belt 9th Kyu
Yellow Belt 8th Kyu
Orange Belt 7th Kyu
Blue Belt 6th–5th Kyu
Green Belt 4th–3rd Kyu
Brown Belt 2nd–1st Kyu
Black Belt (Shodan+) 1st Dan & above
Student Resources

Current students can request their JKA membership cards, grading schedules, and kata reference sheets by contacting the federation office. A student handbook in Armenian and Japanese is available at the dojo reception.
